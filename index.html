CEED.ai: Children For Engaged Environmental Design + AI

Teaching curiosity, inspiring reflection, and empowering children to think critically about AI and sustainability

The cards give kids a starting point and concrete examples to work from. The questions engage the senses and make it relevant to their experience.
-Educator Quote

Toolkit Goals:

Encouraging Reflection

Assisting ideation by encouraging reflection and prioritizing empowerment

Focusing on Prototyping

Centering hands-on digital and physical prototyping as a form of idea realization

Accommodating Students

Enable prototyping mode selection to accommodate student preferences

Step 1: Foundational Activities

  • Create a baseline: Discuss examples from daily life
  • Explore real-world impacts of AI and climate change
  • Example Step 1 activities available online

Step 2: Setting the Scene

  • Explore the Future: Pick a Postcard From the Future describing an issue caused or worsened by environmental change
  • Follow the postcard prompts to reflect on who's affected and why it matters

Step 3: Assembling a Team

  • Spark Ideas: Choose superhero cards with unique powers to help solve the postcard problem
  • Brainstorm how different characters could work together

Step 4: Prototyping and Evaluation

  • Bring ideas to life: Sketch and build physical prototype (use cardboard, markers, straws, etc.)
  • Create digital prototype (use software such as TinkerCad)

Step 5: Presenting the Idea

  • Reflection and storytelling: Present and share ideas with educators, family, and peers
  • Discuss what the future could look like with your invention

The CEED.ai Toolkit was created by Gabriela Buraglia and Annette Guan in collaboration with the MS Human-Computer Interaction program and CoDe Craft Lab at Georgia Tech and Decatur Makers, a local Atlanta makerspace.
